Black Rhinos

6

Black rhinos (Diceros bicornis) are a species of rhinoceros found primarily in eastern and southern Africa. Despite their name, they are not truly black; their skin is typically gray or brown, with a characteristic hooked upper lip designed for browsing shrubs and bushes.

  • Physical Characteristics: Smaller than the white rhino, with a pointed, hooked upper lip adapted for browsing on bushes and trees.
  • Habitat: Savannas, grasslands, and dense forests across sub-Saharan Africa.
  • Behavior and Diet: Critically Endangered, with around 5,000 individuals remaining; threatened by poaching for their horns and habitat loss.
  • Conservation Status: Endangered, with around 1,000 individuals; threatened by habitat loss, poaching, disease, and conflict.
  • Conservation Efforts: Anti-poaching efforts, habitat protection, and rhino translocation to safer areas.
