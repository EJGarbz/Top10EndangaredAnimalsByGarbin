Javan Rhinos

5

The Javan rhinoceros (Rhinoceros sondaicus), also known as the Sunda rhinoceros, is a critically endangered species of rhino native to Southeast Asia. It is one of the rarest rhino species in the world, with its entire population now confined to a single protected area in Indonesia.

  • Physical Characteristics: Smallest rhino species in Asia, with a single horn (10 to 27 cm) and gray, loose, armor-like skin.
  • Habitat: Tropical rainforests, now only found in Ujung Kulon National Park, Java, Indonesia.
  • Behavior and Diet: Solitary and elusive; feeds on leaves, shoots, and fruits.
  • Conservation Status: Critically Endangered, with around 75 individuals left; threatened by habitat loss and natural disasters.
  • Conservation Efforts: Strict protection in Ujung Kulon National Park and habitat restoration projects.
