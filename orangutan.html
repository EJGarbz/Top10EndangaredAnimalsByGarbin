Tapanuli Orangutan

4

The Tapanuli orangutan (Pongo tapanuliensis) is a critically endangered species of orangutan found only in the Batang Toru forest of North Sumatra, Indonesia. Discovered as a distinct species in 2017, it is the rarest and most endangered great ape in the world.

Hover Me!

  • Physical Characteristics: Smallest orangutan species, with frizzier hair and a distinctively long call in males.
  • Habitat: Restricted to the Batang Toru forest in North Sumatra, Indonesia.
  • Behavior and Diet: Solitary, mostly arboreal; feeds on fruits, leaves, and insects
  • Conservation Status: Critically Endangered, with fewer than 800 individuals; threatened by deforestation, mining, and habitat fragmentation.
  • Conservation Efforts: Habitat protection, anti-deforestation campaigns, and efforts to prevent human-wildlife conflict.
