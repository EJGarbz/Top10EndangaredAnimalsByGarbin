Sumatran Orangutans

9

The Sumatran orangutan (Pongo abelii) is a critically endangered species of orangutan native to the island of Sumatra in Indonesia. It is one of the three species of orangutans and is known for its intelligence, reddish-brown fur, and predominantly arboreal lifestyle.

  • Physical Characteristics: Reddish-brown fur, longer face, and a more rounded body compared to other orangutans.
  • Habitat: Tropical rainforests on the island of Sumatra, Indonesia.
  • Behavior and Diet: Solitary, intelligent; feeds on fruits, leaves, and insects, and uses tools.
  • Conservation Status: Critically Endangered, with around 14,000 individuals remaining; threatened by habitat loss, illegal hunting, and deforestation for palm oil plantations.
  • Conservation Efforts: Habitat protection, rehabilitation programs for rescued orangutans, and efforts to reduce deforestation and promote sustainable palm oil production.
