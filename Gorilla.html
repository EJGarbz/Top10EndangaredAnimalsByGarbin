The mountain gorilla (Gorilla beringei beringei) is a subspecies of the eastern gorilla, known for inhabiting mountainous forest regions in East-Central Africa. It is critically endangered and famous for its strength, intelligence, and close genetic relationship to humans.

  • Physical Characteristics: Small, pale golden coat with large rosettes; long fur for cold climates.
  • Habitat: Temperate forests in the Russian Far East and northeastern China.
  • Behavior and Diet: Solitary, nocturnal; preys on deer and smaller animals.
  • Conservation Status: Critically Endangered; fewer than 120 wild individuals due to habitat loss, poaching, and prey depletion.
  • Conservation Efforts: Anti-poaching measures, habitat restoration, and captive breeding programs.
