Yangtze Finless Porpoise

3

The Yangtze finless porpoise (Neophocaena asiaeorientalis ssp. asiaeorientalis) is a freshwater subspecies of finless porpoise found exclusively in the Yangtze River system in China. Known for its playful behavior and unique "smiling" appearance, it is critically endangered.

  • Physical Characteristics: Small, sleek, gray body with no dorsal fin; "smiling" appearance.
  • Habitat: Lives in the Yangtze River and nearby estuaries in China.
  • Behavior and Diet: Social, uses echolocation; feeds on fish and shrimp.
  • Conservation Status: Critically Endangered, with fewer than 1,000 individuals; threatened by habitat loss, pollution, and fishing.
  • Conservation Efforts: Protected reserves, research programs, and public awareness campaigns.
