Hawksbill Turtle

10

The hawksbill turtle (Eretmochelys imbricata) is a critically endangered species of sea turtle known for its sharp, hawk-like beak and beautiful, patterned shell. It is found in tropical and subtropical waters around the world.

Hover Me!

  • Physical Characteristics: Small to medium-sized, with a distinctive, hawk-like beak and a shell with intricate patterns.
  • Habitat: Tropical and subtropical oceans, especially around coral reefs.
  • Behavior and Diet: Primarily feeds on sponges, jellyfish, and sea anemones; often found in coral reefs and coastal areas.
  • Conservation Status: Critically Endangered, with fewer than 25,000 nesting females remaining; threatened by illegal trade of their shells.
  • Conservation Efforts: Protection of nesting sites, international trade regulations, and efforts to reduce bycatch through sustainable fishing practices.
