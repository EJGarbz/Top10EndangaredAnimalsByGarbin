The African forest elephant (Loxodonta cyclotis) is a smaller and more elusive species of elephant found in the rainforests of central and West Africa. It was once considered a subspecies of the African elephant but was recognized as a distinct species in the 1990s due to differences in genetics, size, and behavior.

  • Physical Characteristics: Smaller than the African savanna elephant, with straighter tusks and rounder ears.
  • Habitat: Dense rainforests of central and West Africa.
  • Behavior and Diet: Solitary or in small groups; feeds on fruits, leaves, bark, and other vegetation.
  • Conservation Status: Critically Endangered, with fewer than 50,000 individuals remaining; threatened by habitat destruction, poaching for ivory, and human-wildlife conflict.
  • Conservation Efforts: Habitat protection, anti-poaching laws, and efforts to prevent illegal ivory trade.
